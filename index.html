Thunder Finance —
Decentralized Proof of Credit Score

Thunder Finance is a DeFi protocol designed to make uncollateralized crypto lending viable at scale. Instead of requiring borrowers to lock up assets as collateral, a barrier that excludes most real-world credit demand, DPCS replaces collateral with a structured, privacy-preserving credit assessment process powered by independent validators and verified institutional data.

The core innovation is DPCS: Decentralized Proof of Credit Score. It bridges traditional credit infrastructure with: rating agencies, banks, compliance bureaus, integrating it with on-chain lending mechanics. Borrowers prove their creditworthiness. Validators, who stake their own capital, evaluate each proposal independently and are financially accountable for the credits they approve. Investors verify five most trustable Validators evaluations and decide to fund the loan. Smart Contract executes itself and the credit journey begins.

DPCS exists because the gap between DeFi's capital efficiency and traditional credit markets remains largely unsolved. The protocol is built to close that gap filling it: compliantly, transparently, and without sacrificing the decentralization that makes DeFi worth building on.

The security deposit functions as a collateral reserve. Each loan approval requires the validator to lock 10% of the approved loan amount. A $10K deposit allows participation in up to $100K in total approved loan volume simultaneously. For example, approving a $50K loan locks $5K, leaving $5K available for additional deals. Locked collateral is released progressively as loan installments are paid.

Validators are not assigned — they self-select which proposals to analyze. Any credentialed validator may review and score a proposal at any time. The five highest-ranked validators who approved a given proposal are the ones visible to the investor at the time of funding. If a higher-ranked validator approves after a lower-ranked one, the lower-ranked validator may be displaced and their locked collateral returned for reuse.

No. Each validator submits their analysis independently at their own discretion. Scores are not shared among validators. Only the investor has visibility into the five top-ranked validators' scores for a given proposal. This structure prevents coordination and ensures independent credit assessment.

The ranking bonus is reputational, not financial. Validators who consistently approve credits that are fully repaid improve their ranking over time. A higher ranking increases a validator's priority positioning in proposals and unlocks access to greater deal flow — it does NOT generate a direct cash payment at settlement.

Validators undergo a full onboarding process including KYC, AML, and PEP screening before being granted access to credit proposals. Their credentials and professional competencies are verified against recognized regulatory entities or information bureaus. This ensures that only qualified, auditable participants can act in the protocol.

Borrower credentials are stored durably so that returning borrowers do not need to repeat the full onboarding process for subsequent loan applications. Validators are subject to ongoing compliance monitoring as part of their participation in the protocol.

The protocol is designed to be Travel Rule compatible. Screened and credentialed participants, combined with on-chain audit trails and off-chain encrypted data, allow the protocol to satisfy originator and beneficiary information requirements without exposing PII on-chain. DPCS is aligned with VASP Privacy-Preserving frameworks.

Smart contract audits are a core part of the DPCS security framework. The protocol targets 3 or more independent security audits, formal verification of lending logic, and a bug bounty program on ImmuneFi. Audit timelines are aligned with the testnet and mainnet launch roadmap.

DPCS is evaluating deployment on leading EVM-compatible networks with strong institutional support and low transaction costs. The final chain selection will be informed by ecosystem partnerships and infrastructure readiness.

DPCS is designed to serve companies seeking capital in a more efficient and less bureaucratic way than traditional debt markets — comparable to tokenized debt issuance but at lower cost and with greater accessibility for both issuers and investors. Individual borrowers and SMEs may also be served as the protocol matures.

The protocol earns through two mechanisms: an origination fee charged on each loan, collected proportionally over the installment payment flow; and a yield generated on validator security deposits staked within the protocol. This creates a clean, sustainable revenue model without dependence on interest rate spread.
